# Lenny Ball — A Life in the Groove

Lenny Ball has always made music the way good R&B and soul are meant to be made: in the room, with people, listening closely and leaving space for the groove to settle in.

Over the years, Lenny has brought bass guitar, upright bass, guitar, and vocals to stages and sessions throughout New England. He has played in groups including Lenny and the Latest, Driving Sideways, and The Professor Harp Band. The names matter, but the through line is Lenny: a musician whose sound is built on feel, friendship, and the pleasure of making a song come alive together.

One especially memorable regular stop was the Thursday Night Blues Jam at the Acton Jazz Cafe in Acton, Massachusetts. Week after week, it was a place to show up, plug in, trade a few songs, and be part of the local blues community.

Along the way, Lenny shared music with musicians including Billy Kane, BJ Magoon, Professor Harp, Jimmy Atkinson, Chip, and Pete Henderson, and crossed paths with the Bobby Watson Band. His story is still being gathered—because the best musical stories are rarely held in one place. They live in old flyers, recordings, photographs, club rooms, and the people who were there.

Today, Lenny may be past the performance circuit, but the music has not left him. A basement session with friends, an upright bass in the corner, a guitar within reach, and a song worth trying one more time: that is still home.
