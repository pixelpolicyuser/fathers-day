# Lenny Ball — A Life in the Groove

Lenny Ball is a dedicated R&B, soul, blues, and roots musician and bassist based in New England, especially the Massachusetts area around Mendon. His musical reputation, as remembered by family and reflected in public listings, is built around reliable groove, versatility, and a long-standing presence in the local live music scene.

Family memories highlight Lenny playing bass guitar, upright bass, guitar, and vocals across R&B, soul, blues, and roots traditions. Just as important as the performances was the communal joy of music-making: friends in the room, a song worth trying again, and a rhythm section that let everybody else breathe.

His musical circle included collaborations and friendships with musicians such as Billy Kane, B.J. Magoon, Professor Harp, Jimmy Atkinson, Chip, and Pete Henderson, the longtime frontman of the Bobby Watson Band. Bands and projects associated with Lenny include Lenny and the Latest, Driving Sideways / Drivin’ Sideways, and The Professor Harp Band.

Public records from the early 2000s show Lenny as a core member of the house band for the popular Thursday night blues jams at the Acton Jazz Cafe in Acton, Massachusetts. Archived Worcester Phoenix listings from 2001 publicly name Lenny Ball alongside B.J. Magoon and Mike Avery as hosts of the Thursday blues jams. A 2002 Milford Daily News arts calendar also lists B.J. Magoon, Mike Avery, and Lenny Ball as the Acton Jazz Cafe house band.

Family-supplied public-post context adds that Lenny described more than twenty years of blues experience overall, electric and upright bass work, and about five years in the Acton Jazz Cafe house band with B.J. Magoon / Driving Sideways and Mike Avery. The Thursday night Acton residency stands out as a documented high point of his community engagement in the MetroWest and Boston-area blues scene.

His activity spanned from roughly the late 1990s into the late 2010s, with performances across New England clubs and venues. Even in semi-retirement, music has remained close. In 2026, family found Lenny posting in local musician groups seeking an experienced drummer for a low-key Mendon-based band focused on country, old rock, and blues, with local rehearsals, occasional gigs, and an emphasis on no drama and music first.

Lenny comes across as a steadfast groove player: someone who prioritized live collaboration, jam sessions, and supporting the song over chasing the spotlight. His story is the working musician’s life of late nights, road gigs, club rooms, and basement jams. Today, with music still at home in his Music Room, this biography site serves as a heartfelt tribute from family.
