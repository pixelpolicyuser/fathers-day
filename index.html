Lenny’s Story

A musician’s life leaves traces in club rooms, late-night jams, old friends, and the songs that still call someone back to a chair in the basement. This is a living story—some parts documented in public, some held by the people who were there.

Family memory

Always in the groove

Lenny Ball has played bass guitar, upright bass, guitar, and vocals in the R&B, soul, and blues traditions. Family remembers him making music throughout New England, where the pleasure was as much in the people around the song as the stage itself.

Family memory

Groups, friends, and the road

His musical path includes Lenny and the Latest, Driving Sideways, and The Professor Harp Band, alongside friends and fellow musicians including Billy Kane, BJ Magoon, Professor Harp, Jimmy Atkinson, Chip, and Pete Henderson.

Verified public context

Thursday night in Acton

The Thursday Night Blues Jam at the Acton Jazz Cafe was a long-running local gathering with a documented history of live blues performances and community musicians. Family remembers it as one of Lenny’s regular big shows.

Verified public context

The Bobby Watson Band

Public sources identify Pete Henderson as the longtime frontman of the Bobby Watson Band. This gives a bit of wider context to one of the musicians in Lenny’s musical circle.

Family memory

The music is still home

Today the stage may be quieter, but the room is not: an upright bass, a guitar, a few friends, and another song to try is still the right kind of evening.
