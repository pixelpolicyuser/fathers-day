Lenny’s Story

A musician’s life leaves traces in club rooms, late-night jams, old friends, and the songs that still call someone back to a chair in the basement. This is a living story—some parts documented in public, some held by the people who were there.

Family memory

Always in the groove

Lenny Ball is a New England R&B, soul, blues, and roots musician whose sound has always been about feel first: reliable groove, warm support, and the kind of bass line that gives everybody else room to shine. Family remembers him on bass guitar, upright bass, guitar, and vocals, carrying the music with steadiness and heart.

Family memory

Friends, bands, and basement sessions

His musical path includes Lenny and the Latest, Driving Sideways / Drivin’ Sideways, and The Professor Harp Band, alongside friends and fellow musicians including Billy Kane, B.J. Magoon, Professor Harp, Jimmy Atkinson, Chip, and Pete Henderson. The through line was always the same: the shared joy of playing with people who loved the song.

Verified public context

Thursday night at the Acton Jazz Cafe

Archived Worcester Phoenix listings from 2001 publicly name Lenny Ball alongside B.J. Magoon and Mike Avery as hosts of the Acton Jazz Cafe’s Thursday blues jams. Those listings turn a family memory into a public record: Lenny was part of the weekly blues community in Acton, Massachusetts.

Verified public context

A MetroWest blues fixture

A 2002 MetroWest arts calendar also lists B.J. Magoon, Mike Avery, and Lenny Ball as the Acton Jazz Cafe house band. Together, the public listings place Lenny in the early-2000s New England club scene, connected to blues, R&B, and roots music across local stages.

Family-supplied public memory

Five years in the house band

In a musician-group comment shared by family, Lenny described more than twenty years of blues experience, playing electric and upright bass, and spending about five years in the Acton Jazz Cafe house band with B.J. Magoon / Driving Sideways and Mike Avery.

Verified public context

The Bobby Watson Band

Public sources identify Pete Henderson as the longtime frontman of the Bobby Watson Band. That gives wider context to one of the musicians in Lenny’s circle and the broader regional blues ecosystem around him.

Community / public post

Still chasing the right pocket

As recently as 2026, family found Lenny posting in local musician circles about a low-key Mendon-area band looking for an experienced drummer: country, old rock, blues, rehearsals close to home, occasional gigs, no drama, and music first. It sounds exactly like Lenny: still listening for the right groove.

Family memory

The music is still home

Today the big stage may be quieter, but the room is not: an upright bass, a guitar, a few friends, and another song to try is still the right kind of evening. Lenny’s Music Room is a tribute to the working musician’s life—late nights, road gigs, jam sessions, and the basement songs that keep everything alive.
